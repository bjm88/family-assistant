Avi AI Family Assistant — Privacy Policy

Effective date: April 19, 2026
Operator: Avi AI Family Assistant, a sole proprietorship operated by Benjamin Maisano
Contact: benjamin.maisano@gmail.com

1. Who we are

Avi AI Family Assistant (“Avi”, “we”, “us”, or the “Service”) is a private, household-only AI assistant operated as a sole proprietorship by Benjamin Maisano. The Service is not offered to the general public and is used exclusively by registered members of the operator’s household and pre-authorized invitees.

2. Scope of this policy

This Privacy Policy explains how the Service collects, uses, stores, and protects information when household members interact with Avi through the web admin console, the live chat / face-recognition page, registered email accounts, and SMS / MMS via Twilio. By using the Service, or by exchanging text messages with our Twilio number, you consent to this Policy.

3. Information we collect

We collect only the minimum information necessary to operate a household assistant:

  • Identity & account data. Names, dates of birth, family relationships, and household roles you (or another household admin) enter into the admin console.
  • Contact data. Email addresses and mobile, home, and work phone numbers registered on a household member’s profile. Phone numbers are required to associate inbound SMS / MMS with a known household member.
  • Conversations & transcripts. The text of chat, email, and SMS messages exchanged with Avi, plus the assistant’s replies. SMS transcripts include the sender’s phone number, the body of each text, and any MMS attachments.
  • Operational & audit data. Timestamps, message identifiers (e.g., Twilio MessageSid), opt-out events, delivery status, and per-message security verdicts.
  • Sensitive household data you choose to store. Identification documents, financial-account hints, medical conditions, and similar records you voluntarily upload. These are encrypted at rest using application-level encryption.

We do not collect precise geolocation, advertising identifiers, browser fingerprints, or third-party tracking pixels.

4. How we use information

We use the information above only to:

  • Deliver responses to your messages and execute tasks you have asked the assistant to track.
  • Identify the speaker on inbound SMS, email, or live-chat sessions so we can respond to the correct household member.
  • Maintain an audit trail of every inbound message we received and the outcome (delivered, ignored, or failed).
  • Operate, debug, and improve the Service.

5. Mobile information — required SMS disclosures

No mobile information will be shared with third parties or affiliates for marketing or promotional purposes. All categories of personal information described in this Policy exclude text-messaging originator opt-in data and consent — this information will not be shared with any third parties.

When you send an SMS or MMS to our Twilio number we receive your phone number, the message body, and any MMS media you attach. We use this information solely to:

  • Verify that the sender is a registered household member.
  • Generate and deliver an automated reply.
  • Maintain the audit trail described above.

You may opt out of further SMS interactions at any time by texting STOP to our Twilio number. Twilio handles the carrier-level opt-out and we will not send you further automated SMS replies. Reply HELP for instructions and contact information. See the Terms of Service for the full SMS program details.

6. Sharing of information

We do not sell, rent, or trade your information. We share information only with:

  • Service providers strictly necessary to operate the assistant, currently:
    • Twilio Inc. for SMS / MMS delivery (subject to Twilio’s own privacy practices).
    • Google LLC for Gmail and Calendar integration when you connect a Google account.
    • Local LLM and TTS engines running on the operator’s own hardware. These never transmit data off-device.
  • Law enforcement or regulators if compelled by valid legal process.

In every case, mobile phone numbers and SMS opt-in data are excluded from sharing for marketing or promotional purposes.

7. Data retention

We retain household data for as long as the household member account exists. You may ask us to delete your data at any time by emailing benjamin.maisano@gmail.com. SMS audit rows (sender number, message identifier, status verdict) are retained as part of the operational security log; the body of a message and any MMS media are deleted on request unless retention is required by applicable law.

8. Security

The Service runs on operator-controlled hardware. Sensitive fields (identification numbers, account numbers) are encrypted at rest with Fernet (AES-128-CBC + HMAC-SHA256). Access to the admin console is restricted to the operator and authorized household members. Inbound Twilio webhooks are authenticated using Twilio’s HMAC-SHA1 signature.

9. Your rights

Subject to applicable law you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you.
  • Request correction or deletion of inaccurate data.
  • Opt out of SMS at any time (text STOP).
  • Withdraw consent and request deletion of your records.

To exercise any of these rights, email benjamin.maisano@gmail.com. We will respond within a reasonable period and in any event within 30 days.

10. Children

The Service may include records of minor children belonging to the operator’s household, under the supervision of their parents. The Service is not intended for use by anyone outside the household and is not directed at the general public. We do not knowingly collect data from children outside the household.
