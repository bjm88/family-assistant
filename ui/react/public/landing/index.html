What is Avi?

Avi is a household assistant that runs on your own machine. The admin console captures the structured story of your family — people, relationships, goals, pets, residences, vehicles, insurance, finances, documents, and identity records — and a local AI uses that knowledge to answer questions, recognise family members by face, send reminders, do real-time web research, and keep an eye on the things you ask it to watch. Nothing leaves the box by default.

Key features

Recognises your family on sight

A live camera page detects faces in the browser, then a local InsightFace recogniser names each person and Avi greets them by name in spoken English in around 300 ms.

One assistant, every channel

Live web chat, SMS via Twilio, Telegram, and a dedicated Gmail inbox all land in the same agent loop, so the conversation is the same Avi no matter how you reach it.

Knows your household

People, relationships, goals, pets, residences, vehicles, insurance, finances, documents, and identity records — all first-class data Avi can read so answers are context-aware, not generic.

Real-time web search

Ask casual look-up questions — weather, scores, store hours, current events, recipes — and Avi answers from a live, citation-backed search in a second or two.

Standing watches & reminders

Tell Avi to keep an eye on a flight, a price, a deadline, or a school calendar. Monitoring tasks run on a schedule and ping you when something actually changes.

Local-first & encrypted

Models, faces, and your knowledge base all live on your machine. Sensitive columns (SSNs, account numbers, VINs, license plates) are encrypted at rest with a key only you hold.

Reach Avi from anywhere

The same agent answers across every surface, with the same memory of your household.

Live web chat Streaming replies in the assistant page.
SMS & MMS Twilio number, registered family only.
Email Avi's Gmail inbox, auto-replies in thread.
Telegram Direct chat with the household bot.

Privacy by design. Avi is a private, household-only service. The Service is not offered to the general public — access is limited to registered members of the operator's household and pre-authorised invitees.
